openlectures is a community initiative brought to you by the combined efforts of more than fifty staff and lecturers whose only remuneration is seeing their dreams turn into reality.

Concept

What happens if we take the best of the best and ask them to teach us how they've done it? We'll set up a camera, give them a marker, and throw them in front of a whiteboard. Then we'll take that footage and make it available to the world - for free.

Philosophy

Everyone at openlectures shares two things in common: an intimate grasp of their subject matter, and a fervent passion for sharing their knowledge. Everyone works pro bono, after office hours, and on weekends. Everyone loves what they're doing.

Mission

There's a hundred other e-learning alternatives we can name. Schools use them as supplementary material. Us? We're more than supplements, we're replacements. Look for us if you miss a class, then go back and join up without a worry in the world.

Quality

We've got crazy dedication to quality. Our lectures are checked, peer-checked, then quality-checked. What makes us sure we're teaching the correct thing? We're just sharing what we've learnt.

The Production Line

At least six hours of work goes into a single lecture. Here's what happens when we roll up our sleeves and get down to work.

Forming

In the first stage of planning, we gather a team of students with tremendous exposure and experience in the subject matter. We sit down for coffee. Many rounds of coffee. Our lecturers get comfortable saying no to each other.

Storming

It all starts with an outline. What goes in, what stays out, what's unclear. Everyone looks at that outline, then it's what's good, and what sucks. Fifteen to twenty revisions later, we have a list of lectures.

Norming

Allocation time. Our lecturers know what they know best, so we lay everything out a la mode buffet. Every lecture belongs to someone. From there, we work out the preparation material. More coffee.

Performing

We go down to headquarters at a top secret location. The on-site support team awaits. There's this big, scary camera they use to do the recording. The footage is edited and corrected, then sent for quality checks.
